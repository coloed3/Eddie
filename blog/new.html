Road Map

There are so many good and bad resources for learning web development online. The one I suggest almost to everyone is the one Joe Santos Garcia created. If you dont know who Joe is let me brief you. Joe is a teacher, Senior developer and a Content creator on Youtube and Founder/Owner of Codingphase. His style of teaching is easy to understand. And you Build REAL projects. Projects that you can add to github and use in your portfolios. He is active on youtube and currently is doing a mentorship program where he takes 2 green developers and coaches, criqitues them and lays out the ground work on how to become a developer.Click HERE

On The road map, he breaks it down into 2 categories Front end or back end development. He goes through Every step, has videos on both UDEMY and his site. Follow the time line.

  • 1. Create a schedule
  • 2. Stay motivated, Small wins
  • 3. When Watching or learning something new, Dont be that guy to burn through videos. Put it to work and learn the process. Dont move on until you grasp it.
  • 4. Build a project, your portfolio, sites for a friend get working on a project
  • 5. Sacrifice (time, going out)
  • 6. Invest in you. Buy courses and finish what you start
  • 7. DONT quit, there will times when you feel discouraged stay with it

Follow the road map, ever wonder why people use road maps? Cause the times prior to cell phones one false turn could lead you to a place you never been waisting time in the process. I have heard stories of new developers who start with a bootcamps online or following a road map through another company and they are learning and seeing it all come together. Than decide to take a course for 1200 bucks because its approved by google. Listen Stay on the road stay with the program your on, use codingphase or Udemy colte Steele bootcamp, Brad Traversy, TheNetNinja and finsh what you began
